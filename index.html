About the Lab

The AI and Robotics for Collaborative Systems (ARCS) Lab is a human factors research group in the Department of Industrial & Systems Engineering at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville. We research, design, and promote effective collaboration between humans and intelligent machines to achieve society’s goals. Our work draws from cognitive science, human‑computer interaction, and systems engineering to develop responsible AI teammates that foster trust, shared mental models, and situational awareness.

Application domains include advanced manufacturing, healthcare, emergency response, defense training, and education.

— Dr. Beau Schelble, Founding Director

Research Areas

Human‑AI Teaming

Modeling information‑sharing, team cognition, and workload to create synergistic human–machine partnerships.

Trust & Ethics

Designing transparent AI behaviors and ethical shared mental models that calibrate trust and mitigate compromised teammates.

Training Transference

Improving virtual‑to‑physical skill transference through intelligent interventions and psychological fidelity.

Situational Awareness

Real‑time adaptive interfaces that surface mission‑critical cues and promote collective understanding.

Applied Robotics

Integrating collaborative robots and unmanned ground vehicles into manufacturing and emergency scenarios.

Evaluation & Validation

Developing rigorous experimental frameworks to test AI teammates’ effectiveness, safety, and resilience.
